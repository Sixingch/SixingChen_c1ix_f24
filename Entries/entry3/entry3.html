STREET

The third stop New York City streets are a relentless clash of noise—car horns blaring,
brakes screeching, and voices colliding in an endless hum. Footsteps pound the pavement
as street vendors shout, and the wind rustles trash in alleys. Sirens wail in the distance,
cutting through the chaos. Here, sound isn’t ambiance—it’s aggression, a constant reminder
of the city’s restless energy.
