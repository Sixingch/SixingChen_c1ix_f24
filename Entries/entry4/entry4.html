TIMES SQUARE

The forth stop Times Square, often referred to as "The Cross-roads of the World,"
is one of New York City's most iconic and vibrant locations, known
for its dazzling lights, bustling energy, and rich cultural significance.
Located at the junction of Broadway and Seventh Avenue in Manhattan,
Times Square has long been a central point for both locals and tourists.
The area is renowned for its massive electronic billboards, which light up
the night sky, and its dense concentration of theaters, including the famous
Broadway theaters that showcase world-class performances.

MAX NEUHAUS’ ‘TIMES SQUARE’

This permanent sound installation, created by artist Max Neuhaus in 1977,
emits a deep, bell-like tone from a grate on the pedestrian island at Broadway
between 45th and 46th Streets. The sound originates from an underground vault,
producing a rich harmonic texture that contrasts with the surrounding urban noise.
Neuhaus designed the installation to be subtle and unmarked, encouraging accidental
discovery by passersby. After being removed in 1992, it was reinstalled in 2002 and
continues to offer a moment of unexpected serenity amidst the city's constant activity.
